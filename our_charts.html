Our Analysis on Social Media Usage and Emotional Well-Being

Demographics

The following two dashboards will let you explore different demographic variables, couplings, and distributions. You can take a look at the variables individually. In addition, you can observe grouping information by hovering over each chart and can filter information yourself.

Variables

The attributes used in the demographics section are gender, age, and emotions. In the following dashboard, there are three charts where you can observe the number of social media users from 1,248 records that fit into different categories per variable.

Distribution of Emotions among Social Media Users

In this dashboard, there are three charts you can swap from a drop down menu. Each one will display the distribution of dominant emotions manifested among social media users based on one or two attributes. The first chart will exhibit the distribution of emotions based on the variable, age. Whereas the second one will show the frequency of occurrence of emotions based on gender. Meanwhile, the third chart will present the distribution of emotions based on both attributes, age and gender combined.

Dominant Emotion by Platform

This dashboard allows you to visualize how dominant emotions are distributed across platforms.
To begin, you can hover over a section of the bar chart below to see information about the individual sections of the proportional bar chart.
By clicking or selecting an area of the sections of the bars themselves, the chart below with the blue boxes, will change to show the specific emotions reported and the number of users reporting.

Time Spent on Social Media

This section focus on time in relation to demographic, platform and social media interaction.

In the drop down menu there are 3 types of graphs you can explore:

  • Distribution of Time and Demographics: The bar chart illustrates the amount of time different demographic groups, categorized by age and gender, spend on social media, along with the platforms they use.
  • Distribution of Time and Platform: This tree graph displays the most frequently used social media platforms, showing which platforms users spend the most time on.
  • Distribution of Time and Social Media Interactions: This scatter plot shows three types of interactions (comments, likes, and messages) in relation to the amount of time people spend on social media.

The main graph, 'Dominant Emotion Based on Daily Usage' will stay at the top as you switch between different graph. This graph shows the range of time spent daily associated with each dominant emotion. If you hover over each emotion, the corresponding graphs will update to show data specific to that emotion.

Interaction

This section focuses on the dominant emotions of people based on the metric selected by the user.

There are two types of graphs you can explore:

  • Average Value Across All Ages for Selected Metric: This graph shows the dominant emotions of people across all ages based on the selected metric.
  • Average Value by Age for Selected Metric: This graph displays the dominant emotions for a specific age group based on the selected metric.

The left graph remains unchanged regardless of different age groups because it represents data for all ages. This allows you to compare the left and right graphs to see how the dominant emotions of people in different age groups differ from the overall average.

By comparing these graphs, you can gain insights into how dominant emotions vary with age. The left graph provides a baseline by showing the average for all ages, while the right graph highlights variations specific to different age groups. This comparison can help identify age-related trends or differences in emotional responses based on the selected metric.
